# UK Compliance Requirements

## HMO (House in Multiple Occupation) Regulations

### Definition
A property is an HMO if:
- At least 3 tenants live there, forming more than 1 household
- Toilet, bathroom, or kitchen facilities are shared
- At least one tenant pays rent

### Mandatory HMO Licence
Required for large HMOs (5+ people from 2+ households). Local councils may have additional licensing schemes.

**Licence Duration**: Up to 5 years
**Renewal**: Must apply before expiry

## Compliance Certificates

### 1. Gas Safety Certificate (CP12)
- **Frequency**: Annual (every 12 months)
- **Requirement**: Mandatory for all properties with gas appliances
- **Issued by**: Gas Safe registered engineer
- **Penalty for non-compliance**: Up to £6,000 fine, potential manslaughter charges
- **Must provide to**: Tenants within 28 days of check, new tenants before moving in

### 2. Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)
- **Frequency**: Every 5 years
- **Requirement**: Mandatory for all private rentals (since April 2021)
- **Issued by**: Qualified electrician
- **Penalty**: Up to £30,000 fine
- **Must provide to**: Tenants within 28 days of inspection, prospective tenants within 28 days of request

### 3. Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)
- **Frequency**: Every 10 years
- **Requirement**: Mandatory, minimum rating E (some exceptions)
- **Minimum rating**: E (C required for new tenancies from 2025)
- **Must provide to**: Prospective tenants before viewing

### 4. Fire Risk Assessment
- **Frequency**: Annually recommended
- **Requirement**: Mandatory for HMOs with common areas
- **Covers**: Escape routes, fire detection, fire-fighting equipment

### 5. Legionella Risk Assessment
- **Frequency**: Every 2 years (or when water systems change)
- **Requirement**: Mandatory for all landlords
- **Issued by**: Can be self-assessed or professional

### 6. Smoke & CO Alarms
- **Requirement**: Mandatory since October 2022
- **Smoke alarms**: Every floor
- **CO alarms**: Any room with fixed combustion appliance (excluding gas cookers)
- **Testing**: At start of each tenancy

### 7. PAT Testing (Portable Appliance Testing)
- **Frequency**: Annually recommended
- **Requirement**: Not legally mandatory but demonstrates due diligence
- **Covers**: All electrical appliances provided by landlord

### 8. Asbestos Survey
- **Requirement**: Properties built before 2000 should have survey
- **No expiry**: Unless building works disturb materials
- **Must provide to**: Anyone who may disturb asbestos-containing materials

## HMO-Specific Requirements

### Room Sizes (England)
- **Single adult (18+)**: 6.51 m² minimum
- **Two adults**: 10.22 m² minimum
- **Child (10-18)**: 4.64 m² minimum
- **Under 10**: Can share with one parent

### Facilities
- **Kitchen**: 1 cooker, sink, worktop, food storage per 5 people
- **Bathroom**: 1 bathroom/toilet per 5 people
- **Living space**: Adequate communal areas for number of occupants

### Fire Safety
- Fire doors to bedrooms and kitchens
- Fire extinguishers in common areas
- Emergency lighting in large HMOs
- Fire blankets in kitchens

## Renters' Rights Act 2025

### Key Changes

#### 1. Section 21 Abolition
- No-fault evictions abolished
- All tenancies become periodic (no fixed terms enforced)
- Landlords must use Section 8 grounds for possession

#### 2. Private Rented Sector (PRS) Database
- Mandatory registration for all landlords
- Public database of compliant landlords
- Non-registration = illegal to let

#### 3. Tenant Information Sheet
- Mandatory document for all new tenancies
- Standard format prescribed by government
- Covers tenant rights, landlord obligations

#### 4. Pet Rights
- Tenants can request pets
- Landlords can't unreasonably refuse
- Can require pet damage insurance

#### 5. Decent Homes Standard
- Social housing standards applied to private rentals
- Enforcement through local councils

### Implementation Timeline
- Phased rollout beginning 2025
- PRS Database expected late 2025/early 2026
- Full Section 21 abolition expected 2026

## Deposit Protection

### Requirements
- Must protect within 30 days of receipt
- Must provide Prescribed Information within 30 days
- Use government-approved scheme

### Approved Schemes
- Deposit Protection Service (DPS)
- MyDeposits
- Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS)

### Penalties
- Failure to protect: 1-3x deposit in compensation
- Cannot serve Section 21 (while still exists) without protection
